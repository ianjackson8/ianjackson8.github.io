About Me

My story starts in the small town Union, WV, population 459. During my time at James Monroe High School, I was involved in Model United Nations (MUN), National Honor Society (NHS), soccer and robotics. In each of these clubs, I strived to go above and beyond. Our MUN team was the only team from West Virginia to attend the annual Georgia Tech MUN conference. In early 2020, I was apart of the first MUN debate team from West Virginia to attend the Harvard World MUN conference.

In 2016, my friends and I started the first 4-H robotics team in the county. We self taught everything we did, from learning how to use CAD to programming the robot using Java. For four years our hard work took us to places such as Scraton, Huntsville, St. Louis, and Detroit. Our success allowed us to attened the World Championship three times (out last time was not attended due to the ongoing pandemic in 2020). Learn more about M-Cubed robotics here.

I graduated from James Monroe High School in 2020 along with 120 classmates (spaced six feet apart from one another). I then went on to start my collegate career at West Virginia University in fall of 2020.
